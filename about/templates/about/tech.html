  • About the technologies

I will not list all the software tools that I needed to create a mini-site backend, but I will still highlight one of them which is necessary, but was not obvious to me at first glance:

  • HTML, CSS - in my opinion, a basic understanding should be mandatory, I had to work hard and dive into the basics. Without knowledge at the initial level, the site will to put it mildly, ugly. I think javascript will also come in handy.

    • Among the tools that I had to use during the creation of the project, there are those that I will highlight among the others.

  • The theory of Practicum is concise, but at the same time informative, holistic. Thanks to the team for the material.

  • The second, but definitely not in importance, is the official Django documentation, where there are answers to all questions, but sometimes you have to suffer. because reading the documentation in the original is not the same as reading it in your native language. Questions often remain. And here I move on to the next point.

  • Stack Overflow - my irreplaceable assistant in debugging the project, I found the answer to almost all questions on this wonderful resource.


    • In general, I have identified one difficulty for myself - a huge, sometimes it seems, just an immense amount of information that you receive daily. Sometimes it seems that the head will explode. And the feeling that there is constantly not enough time.

    Thanks to everyone who read :)
